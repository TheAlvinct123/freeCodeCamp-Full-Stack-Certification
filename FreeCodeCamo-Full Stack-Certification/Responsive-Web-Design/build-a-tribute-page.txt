        <h2 id="tribute-info">Here's a time line of Akira Toriyama:</h2>
        <ul>
          <li><span class="bold">1955:</span> Born April 5 in Kiyosu, Aichi Prefecture, Japan. </li> 
          <li><span class="bold">Early Life:</span> Grew up with a fascination for drawing, particularly animals and vehicles. Inspired by Disney's 101 Dalmatians and his father's interest in motorcycles and auto repair. </li> 
          <li><span class="bold">1977:</span> Began his manga career, submitting works to magazines. </li>
          <li><span class="bold">1978:</span> First published manga, Wonder Island, in Weekly Shōnen Jump, but it didn't gain popularity. </li>
          <li><span class="bold">1980-1984:</span> Achieved major success with Dr. Slump, which ran in Weekly Shōnen Jump. </li>
          <li><span class="bold">1983:</span> Founded Bird Studio. </li>
          <li><span class="bold">1983:</span> Created prototype manga for Dragon Ball: Dragon Boy and The Adventures of Tongpoo. </li>
          <li><span class="bold">1984:</span> Began serialization of Dragon Ball. </li>
          <li><span class="bold">1993:</span> Created the manga Go! Go! Ackman, which was adapted into video games. 
</li>
          <li><span class="bold">1995:</span> Ended the Dragon Ball manga serialization. </li>
          <li><span class="bold">1990s-2000s:</span> Continued working on various manga, character designs, and video game projects. </li>
          <li><span class="bold">2000s:</span> Collaborated on Dragon Quest video game series, designing characters. </li> 
          <li><span class="bold">2013-2022:</span> Worked on Dragon Ball Z: Battle of Gods, Dragon Ball Z: Resurrection F, and Dragon Ball Super. </li>
          <li><span class="bold">2024:</span> Passed away on March 1. </li>
          <li><span class="bold">Legacy:</span> Widely regarded as one of the most influential manga artists of all time, with Dragon Ball achieving global recognition and impact. </li>
        </ul>
        <p id="quote">"Your impact wasn't limited to the manga industry alone. The childhoods of so many creators from several industries were no doubt rooted in the excitement of reading Dragon Ball weekly. Your existence is like a great tree whose branches spread into the sky."
-Eiichiro Oda</p>
